The Haitian Revolution: Founding A Black Nation

The Haitian Revolution was made possible by the ingenuity and skill of French-enslaved Africans just as much as as it was enabled by specific historical circumstances. This piece explores the rebellion that earned Haiti her independence, and argues that its unique implementation could only have been successful in late-18th century Haiti.

The Sociocultural Evolution of Race

Americans often implicitly assume that there is a certain natural endurance of race as a human identification category. However, we do not see the concept of race as we know it appear in its modern form until about 400 years ago. This extensive piece explores the inception and development of racial categorization and what those roots mean for modern identity today.
