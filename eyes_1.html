Smoky Brown and Gold Eyeshadow

What You Need

                      
  • pale (a shade or two lighter than your skin), medium brown (a shade or two darker than your skin), dark brown, and shimmery gold eyeshadow
  • wide flat eyeshadow brush and small thin eyeshaodw brushes
  • wide eyeshadow blending brush and small (crease) eyeshadow blending brush
  • dark brown liquid eyeliner and pale gold eyeliner
  • eyelash curler and mascara

How To Apply

  1. Start by applying an eye makeup primer.
  2. Using one side of the wide flat eyeshadow brush, apply the pale eyeshadow to your upper brow-bone area.
  3. Use one small thin eyeshadow brush to apply the pale shade to your inner eyelid corner.
  4. Using the clean back side of the wide flat eyeshadow brush, apply your medium brown shadow above your crease, starting in a thin line above the inner corner of your eye and ending wider above the outer corner.
  5. Blend the line between the pale and medium shades with the wide eyeshadow blending brush so that the transition with a gradient effect rather than a clearly defined line.
  6. Use a small thin eyeshadow brush to apply the dark brown eyeshadow to your deep crease, outr corner, and lower lash line.
  7. Blend dark shade into medium shadow and your middle eyelid to achieve a gradient fade effect with the small (crease) eyeshadow blending brush.
  8. Use your pinky finger to apply the shimmery gold to your middle eyelid, linking the pale inner corner with the dark outer corner (the heat from your finger helps to better apply shimmery pigments).
  9. Apply dark brown liquid eyeliner in a wing shape starting just before you iris and swinging out to your outer corner (thickest lines should be at the outer corner).
  10. Add pale gold eyeliner to inner corners, then curl eyelashes and apply mascara.
